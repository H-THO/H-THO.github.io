Emotional Entelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (or quotient - EQ) is a set of skills in our perception and processing of social and emotional information. We can break this down into four major skillsets or abilities (read more):

  1. Perceiving emotions.
  2. Reasoning with emotions.
  3. Understanding emotions.
  4. Managing emotions.

The general consensus is that 'intelligence' is a complex, broad concept that cannot be measured with a single empirical test for a specific set of traits or abilities. EQ looks more closely at our social skills, ability to manage and regulate emotions, and how we empathise with others.

How is it different to IQ?

General measures of intellectual ability usually revolve around empirical measures of an individuals ability to Learn, Recognize problems and solve them..

IQ tests tend to measure thetestakers abilities using logic, reasoning, problem-solving and planning. There has been debate over the level of cultural bias and narrow focus of such testing, and it's relevance to the future success of test subjects.

Emotional Intelligence Quotient (EQ) seeks to recognize abilities not traditionally considered by general measures of IQ, and focus more on our abilities to understand our emotions and those of others.

This EI skillset could be broken down into 5 categories:

  1. Self-Awareness - Ability to understand and recognize own emotions.
  2. Self-Regulation - Ability to regulate when and how we express these emotions appropriately.
  3. Social Skills - Ability to interact well with others.
  4. Empathy - Ability to understand how others are feeling, and your response to this.
  5. Motivation - Ability to align motivation with internal reward, beyond fame/money/recognition.

Why is it important?

Contemporary research has shown that EQ may even be a significant factor in determining professional success.

While the traditional model of IQ testing gave great weight to intellectual or academic ability, EQ can tell us a great deal of a persons ability to succeed and gain personal fulfillment.

A holistic approach to measuring intelligence should always include consideration of EQ based skills.
